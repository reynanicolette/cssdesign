Designing for Web

Know Your Audience

If a Creative Brief is provided, understand the client's wishes for the website. What's the mood for the website? Is branding already created? You know how to answer these questions for print pieces, so use the brief to figure that out.

Look for Inspiration

The subject of your site is a good place to start for inspiration. See what businesses are doing. Making a restaurant website? Find websites of similar cuisine and price range.

You don't always have to stick to subject to find a website that will help you.

Some inspiration sites

  2. Dribbble
  3. Behance

Plan your branding ahead of time

You can create a separate artboard for this, and add heading styles, paragraph styles, shadow styles, border styles, link styles, button styles, hover states, etc. This allows you to plan better.

Sketch Example Layouts

These wireframes can help give you ideas of what layouts you want without committing to one while you design.

Communicate with your Client!

In a web course, your instructor is your client, so it's a good idea to consult your ideas with them before you officially start the design. If you follow a path that may not be ideal, figuring it out in advance will help you out!
